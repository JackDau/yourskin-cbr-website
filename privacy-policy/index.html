Privacy Policy

YourGP Privacy Policy — Reviewed February 2026

Download PDF

1. Introduction

This privacy policy is to provide information to you on how your personal information (which includes your health information) is collected and used by your doctor and by staff at YourGP, and the circumstances in which we may share it with third parties.

For enquiries concerning this policy, contact a manager through the contact details provided at the end of this document.

2. Why and When Your Consent Is Necessary

When you register as a patient, you provide consent for GPs and staff to access and use your personal information so they can provide you with the best possible healthcare. Only staff and clinicians who need to see your personal information will access it. If we need to use your information for anything else, we will seek additional consent from you to do this.

3. Why Do We Collect, Use, Hold and Share Your Personal Information?

YourGP collects, uses, stores, and shares your personal information primarily to manage your health safely and effectively. This includes providing healthcare services, managing medical records, and ensuring accurate billing and payments.

Additionally, we may utilise your information for internal quality and safety improvement processes such as practice audits, accreditation purposes, and staff training to maintain high-quality service standards.

4. What Personal Information Do We Collect?

The types of personal information we collect include:

  • Names, date of birth, addresses, contact details
  • Medical information including medical history, medications, allergies, adverse events, immunisations, social history, family history and risk factors
  • Medicare number (where available)
  • Healthcare identifiers
  • Clinical photographs (taken with your consent)

5. Dealing With Us Anonymously

You have the right to deal with your doctor and YourGP anonymously or under a pseudonym, unless it is not practical for us to do so, or unless required or authorised by law to only deal with identified individuals.

6. How Is Personal Information Collected?

Your personal information may be collected in a number of ways, including:

  • When you complete a registration form at the practice
  • Via our website, email, or SMS communications
  • From your guardian or responsible person
  • From other healthcare providers (such as specialists or hospitals)
  • From your health fund, Medicare, or DVA
  • Through electronic prescribing, My Health Record, or online appointment systems
  • Via CCTV footage captured for security purposes (not in consultation rooms)

7. When, Why and With Whom Do We Share Your Personal Information?

We may share your personal information with the following parties in certain circumstances:

  • Third parties involved in accreditation or IT support
  • Other healthcare providers involved in your care
  • When required by law
  • Where there is a serious threat to safety
  • In the case of missing persons
  • In support of equitable claims

Only those individuals who need access to your information will be granted it. Some administrative staff based in Malaysia may access your information as part of their duties. We will not share your information overseas without your consent.

8. Will Your Information Be Used for Marketing Purposes?

We will not use your personal information for marketing purposes without your express consent. You may opt out in writing at any time. Educational emails may be sent periodically.

9. How Are Artificial Intelligence (AI) Scribes Used?

Where AI scribes are used during consultations, the following safeguards are in place:

  • Informed consent is obtained for each individual consultation and is not carried forward
  • Audio is recorded solely for the purpose of generating clinical notes
  • No data is stored or processed outside Australia
  • Audio recordings are destroyed after transcription is complete
  • Personally identifiable information (PII) is removed from transcriptions

10. How Do We Store and Protect Your Personal Information?

We take reasonable steps to protect your personal information from misuse, interference, and loss, as well as unauthorised access, modification, or disclosure. Your information is stored in electronic medical records and, where applicable, in paper records stored securely. Measures include locked files, secure IT systems, and confidentiality agreements for all staff.

11. How Can You Access and Correct Your Personal Information Held by Us?

You have the right to request access to, or correction of, your personal information held by us. Requests should be made in writing. We aim to respond within 30 days. An administration fee may apply for processing access requests. We will take reasonable steps to correct any information that is found to be inaccurate, incomplete, or out of date. We may also contact you periodically to verify your information is current.

12. How Can You Lodge a Privacy-Related Complaint?

If you wish to lodge a complaint about how we have handled your personal information, you can contact us through the following channels:

  • Email: admin@ygp.au
  • Phone: (02) 6109 0000
  • Mail:
    • Crace: 1/5 Baratta St, Crace ACT 2911
    • Denman Prospect: 1B Felstead Vista, Denman Prospect ACT 2611
    • Lyneham: 1/62 Brigalow St, Lyneham ACT 2602

We aim to respond to all complaints within 30 days.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC):

  • Website: www.oaic.gov.au
  • Phone: 1300 363 992

13. Policy Review Statement

This privacy policy is regularly reviewed to ensure it remains current and compliant with applicable legislation. Any changes will be reflected on our website. Where significant changes are made, we will communicate these directly to our patients.
